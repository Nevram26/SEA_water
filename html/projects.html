SDG6 Projects
Working towards clean water and sanitation for all.

Angat Water Supply System Expansion Project

The project aims to ensure the raw water supply for 13 million residents in the Metropolitan Waterworks and Sewerage System (MWSS) service area by rehabilitating the Angat transmission line. Currently, approximately 800,000 cubic meters (m³) or 20% of the total potential raw water capacity is lost daily due to aqueduct leaks. The primary components of the Angat transmission line, which are up to 50 years old, are in poor condition and do not meet structural and seismic standards, posing a risk of partial water supply interruption in Metro Manila. The proposed funding method is a project loan.

Learn more: Asian Development Bank

Water Supply and Sanitation for the Urban Poor Project in Metro Manila

This project, implemented by the Manila Water Company, provides safe drinking water and sanitation services to low-income communities in Metro Manila. The project includes upgrading existing water supply systems, constructing new sanitation facilities, and promoting hygiene awareness.

Learn more: Philippine Center for Investigative Journalism

Water Supply and Sanitation Project for Indigenous Peoples in Bukidnon

This project, funded by the European Union, supports indigenous communities in Bukidnon province in accessing clean water and sanitation services. The project involves drilling deep wells, constructing water supply systems, and providing training and education on hygiene practices.

Learn more: Hope NZ
