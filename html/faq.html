Frequently Asked Questions
What is SDG 6?

SDG 6, part of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, aims to ensure the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030. It addresses issues such as water scarcity, water quality, sanitation access, and ecosystem protection.

Why is SDG 6 important?

Access to clean water and proper sanitation is fundamental to human health and development. It prevents diseases, supports sustainable ecosystems, and promotes dignity and safety. Achieving SDG 6 is critical for reducing poverty and building resilient communities.

What are the main challenges to achieving SDG 6?

The main challenges include water scarcity, pollution, inadequate infrastructure, poor sanitation, and lack of awareness. Climate change, population growth, and industrialization also place additional strain on water resources.

How can I contribute to achieving SDG 6?

You can contribute by supporting NGOs working on water and sanitation issues, participating in local clean-up drives, conserving water, spreading awareness, and advocating for policies that promote sustainable water management.

Where do my donations go?

Donations go directly to funding projects and initiatives focused on providing clean water, improving sanitation facilities, and educating communities on hygiene practices. We work with trusted NGOs to ensure that funds are used effectively and transparently.

How are the projects monitored and evaluated?

Our projects are regularly monitored through field visits, progress reports, and community feedback. We collaborate with local partners to ensure sustainability and measure the impact through key performance indicators such as the number of people with improved access to water and sanitation.

How can businesses get involved in supporting SDG 6?

Businesses can support SDG 6 by implementing sustainable water management practices, reducing their water footprint, partnering with NGOs, and supporting community initiatives related to water and sanitation.
